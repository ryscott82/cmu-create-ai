Creators & CMU CREATE Lab

The AI Fluency Pilot Project collaboration combines CMU's world-class research and pedagogical expertise with STEM Coding Lab's (SCL) proven track record of regional delivery. The CREATE Lab applies technology and collaboration to tackle societal concerns, including enhancing classroom learning.

Valley School of Ligonier's technology teachers, Ruth Barton and Reneé Moore, are excited to be part of the AI Fluency Pilot Project. "When one of my second grade students complained that her family's electric-powered vehicle was creating frustration because its outdated GPS maps were causing them to get lost... it sparked classroom discussions about how AI and technology play a role in our daily lives," Barton said.

"Partnering with the CMU CREATE Lab and STEM Coding Lab to pilot these modules will prepare our students for careers of the future by guiding them to think critically, responsibly and creatively about how technology shapes the world around them," Moore added.
